## You Don't Have to Do It Alone

If you've followed this course, you now know everything you need to pack your home yourself. But here's the truth we tell every customer: you don't have to do it all. Professional packing exists for a reason, and sometimes the smartest move is letting someone else handle part — or all — of the job.

## When to Consider Professional Packers

### Time pressure

- You're working full-time and can't take days off to pack
- The completion date was brought forward and you have less time than planned
- You're juggling childcare, work, and the move simultaneously

### Physical limitations

- You have a back injury, mobility issues, or are recovering from surgery
- You're pregnant or have recently had a baby
- You're managing a move for elderly parents who can't pack themselves

### Complexity

- You have high-value items — artwork, antiques, musical instruments
- The home is large (4+ bedrooms) and the volume is overwhelming
- You're moving long-distance and need everything packed to withstand a longer journey

### Stress

- Honestly? Some people just don't want to pack. And that's completely fine. If the stress of packing is keeping you awake at night, hiring professionals removes that entirely.

## What a Professional Packing Service Includes

When you book a [packing service](/home-packing-service) with a removal company, here's what typically happens:

1. **The team arrives the day before your move** (or the morning of, for smaller homes)
2. **They bring all materials** — boxes, paper, bubble wrap, tape, wardrobe boxes, picture boxes. You don't need to buy anything
3. **Every room is packed systematically** — one room at a time, with each box labelled by room and contents
4. **Fragile items get professional wrapping** — china, glass, electronics, artwork. The techniques we've covered in this course, done at speed by people who do it daily
5. **Your home is ready for the van** by the time the packers finish

## Partial Packing

You don't have to choose between packing everything yourself and paying for the whole house. Many customers take a middle approach:

- **Pack the easy rooms yourself** — bedrooms, wardrobes, general storage
- **Let the professionals handle the hard rooms** — kitchen, study, fragile items
- **Focus on specific items** — some customers only want us to pack their china, artwork, or electronics

This hybrid approach reduces the cost while still giving you professional protection where it matters most.

## Getting a Quote

When you request a packing quote, we'll want to know:

- **Home size** — number of bedrooms and approximate volume
- **What needs packing** — full house, specific rooms, or specific items
- **Any specialist items** — pianos, artwork, antiques
- **Access at both properties** — stairs, parking, long carry distances
- **Moving date** — packing usually happens the day before

A home survey (in person or by video) gives us the most accurate picture. We can then quote for packing, removals, or both together.

## Why Customers Choose Professional Packing

- **Speed** — what takes you a week of evenings takes a team a single day
- **Materials included** — no shopping for boxes, no guessing quantities
- **Insurance** — professionally packed items are covered by the removal company's transit insurance. Self-packed items may not be covered to the same level
- **Peace of mind** — you know everything is done properly, and you can focus on the hundred other things that need your attention during a move
